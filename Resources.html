Resources and Presentations¶

The goal of this project is to develop a set of free open source resources to facilitate and promote adherence to the principles of reproducible analysis and literate programming. The web portion of the acts as a resource for training workshops that will teach and promote adherence to reproducible and literate analysis.

In addition to this web-resource we have developed a tool called lpEdit, which is a cross-platform editor that through the use of templates reduces the burden of work as researchers perform the task of literate programming.

The resources available here may be anything related to the integral components of reproducible research: data sharing, establishing an audit trail, and documentation.

Getting started:¶

To get started with literate programming either via lpEdit or using an editor of your choice a version of LaTeX must be installed on your system. Once you have installed your editor and your LaTeX distribution move on to the Tutorials to start creating reproducible documents.
